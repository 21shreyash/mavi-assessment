04 · Leadership

How I'd run this team

Management philosophy, driving performance, handling underperformers, influencing cross-functional partners, and who I'd hire.

Management Philosophy

Hire for strength. Calibrate autonomy. Multiply output.

I'm not interested in managing people. I'm interested in creating conditions where smart, motivated people can do their best work, aligned to what the business needs to win. I hire for superpowers, not the absence of flaws.

  • Autonomy is calibrated, not uniform. New team members on unfamiliar tasks get more structure early; experienced people on known problems get space to run
  • My job is to amplify: ask right questions, clear blockers, make work visible to right stakeholders
  • People need to feel seen and valued, not just measured. Credit flows to the people doing the work
  • Weekly 1:1s are non-negotiable. The agenda is owned by the team member, not me

Driving Performance

Make the goal meaningful, then make it specific.

People don't work hard for metrics. They work hard when they understand why the metrics matter. OKR structure, applied practically.

  • Objective: Build the most trusted finance talent network in the world
  • Key Results: 300 admissions/month by segment · vetter pass rate floor · segment fill rate vs. demand
  • Everyone owns at least one KR. Accountability without ownership is just pressure
  • Real-time feedback: wins called out immediately, course corrections in the moment
Cadence: Daily pipeline check · Weekly funnel review + 1:1s · Monthly quality loop + OKR reset
Underperformers

Care personally. Challenge directly. Never let someone fail slowly.

The worst thing a manager can do is be kind to someone's face while they quietly fail. Feedback is delivered the moment I see a pattern, not saved for a formal process. Every person has their own psychology: underperformance is almost never about effort.

1
Understand first
Genuine conversation, not a warning. What energises them? What does great work feel like from their perspective?
2
Reset the contract
If a pathway exists: reshape the role, clarify success metrics, agree on timeline. Commit to what I'll do differently too.
3
Part ways decisively
If there's no pathway, move quickly and cleanly. Keeping a mismatched person costs the business and the individual.
How I Influence

Influence without authority is the real job.

At seed stage, almost nothing important happens within your direct reporting line. Getting things done means bringing people along through shared interest and mutual trust.

Growth
Built on a data exchange: pass-rate-by-channel to them, sourcing data to me. Joint decisions on quality vs. volume. The dependency is mutual and I make sure both sides feel that.
Product / Engineering
Lead with the problem, make it interesting. At Float: got an LLM pipeline built by framing it as a genuine technical challenge to someone outside my reporting line. Gave him full public credit; he presented at All Hands. He became a committed partner because he felt ownership, not obligation.
Own team
Here's the target, here's why it matters, here's how your work connects to the outcome. Recognition isn't a nice-to-have. It's how people know their work matters.
